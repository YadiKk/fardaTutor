IELTS Test Sections

  1. 30 min

    LISTENING

    4 sections, 40 questions. Academic and everyday contexts.

    • Listen for specific information and main ideas
    • Practice with different accents (British, Australian, American)
    • Read questions before listening
  2. 60 min

    READING

    3 passages, 40 questions. Academic texts and general interest articles.

    • Skim and scan techniques for time management
    • Identify keywords and synonyms
    • Practice with authentic IELTS materials
  3. 60 min

    WRITING

    Task 1: 150 words, Task 2: 250 words. Academic and general formats.

    • Plan your essay structure before writing
    • Use formal academic language and linking words
    • Practice different essay types and graph descriptions
  4. 11-14 min

    SPEAKING

    3 parts: Introduction, Individual long turn, Two-way discussion.

    • Speak naturally and fluently
    • Use a range of vocabulary and grammar structures
    • Practice with sample questions and topics

B1 - Intermediate

IELTS 4.0 - 5.0

Clear communication on familiar matters

B2 - Upper Intermediate

IELTS 5.5 - 6.5

Complex ideas and abstract concepts

C1 - Advanced

IELTS 7.0 - 8.0

Fluent and spontaneous communication

C2 - Proficient

IELTS 8.5 - 9.0

Native-like proficiency and precision

IELTS Preparation Tips

  1. Week 1-2

    ASSESS YOUR CURRENT LEVEL

    Take a practice test to identify your strengths and weaknesses

  2. Week 3-4

    SET REALISTIC GOALS

    Determine your target band score based on your requirements

  3. Week 5-8

    DEVELOP TEST STRATEGIES

    Learn specific techniques for each section and practice regularly

  4. Week 9-12

    MOCK TESTS & FEEDBACK

    Take full practice tests under exam conditions and get professional feedback
